🍪 Cookie Crumbs

A cute guide to understanding web cookies

Part 1: AI Prompts

  1. Prompt 1: Task: Explain what web cookies are. Context: I am a college student in a 200-level CIS class learning about websites and privacy. Tone: Educational, simple, and professional.
  2. Prompt 2: Task: Explain the functions of cookies on websites. Context: I need to understand how cookies help websites remember users. Tone: Friendly, clear, and academic.
  3. Prompt 3: Task: Explain how cookies can create privacy risks. Context: I am completing a cybersecurity-related assignment. Tone: Professional and easy to understand.

Part 2: Understanding Cookies

  1. What Are Cookies?

    Cookies
    Cookies are small text files saved on a user’s browser or device.
    Main Purpose
    They help websites remember information such as login details and user preferences.

  2. Functions of Cookies

    • Keep users logged in
    • Save website preferences
    • Track website activity
    • Support personalized recommendations
    • Help websites collect analytics

  3. Privacy Risks of Cookies

    • Track browsing behavior across websites
    • Collect personal information
    • Support targeted advertising
    • Create privacy concerns if data is shared

Fun Fact: Some cookies improve user experience by remembering your settings and login information!

Part 3: Prompt Evaluation Summary

The AI evaluation explained that my prompts were effective because they clearly included task, context, and tone. The task section told the AI exactly what information I wanted, which helped create accurate responses. The context section explained that the assignment was for a college CIS course, allowing the AI to provide information that was educational and appropriate for students. The tone section also improved the responses because it guided the AI to sound professional, simple, and easy to understand. The feedback suggested that the prompts were organized and detailed enough to avoid confusion. One suggestion for improvement was adding a request for examples so the responses could connect more to real-world websites and cybersecurity situations. Another suggestion was to ask for shorter bullet-point responses to make the information easier to transfer into HTML. Overall, I believe the prompts worked well because they gave the AI clear instructions while still allowing flexibility in the responses. The prompts helped produce accurate and readable information about cookies, their functions, and privacy risks.
